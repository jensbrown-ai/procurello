The shift

From forms to conversation. From reactive to predictive.

Today's procurement systems make casual users navigate eighteen tiles, four shop links, and six form types — and still arrive at AP unprepared. We rebuilt the experience from the user's question backward.

Today
Form-driven, reactive, opaque
  • User has to pick the right form before they begin
  • Catalog vs. non-catalog is the user's problem to solve
  • Red-text disclaimers buried at the bottom of pages
  • Compliance is a manual analyst task after the fact
  • AP gets invoices with no warning, scrambles for receipts
  • Leaders see anomalies only at grant close
→
This concept
Conversational, predictive, transparent
  • One composer accepts text, files, or forwarded emails
  • Agent classifies intent and picks the right procurement object
  • Guardrails surface as gentle nudges during composition
  • 12 compliance checks run silently, exportable audit trail
  • AP sees a 30-day cash flow forecast with confidence intervals
  • Leaders see anomalies 4 months early, with action options
Core capabilities

Six ideas that change the experience.

Each capability addresses a specific failure mode of today's systems. Together they reframe procurement as an intelligent service rather than a compliance exercise.

Conversational entry
One composer accepts plain language, attached quotes, supplier proposals, or forwarded invoices. The agent picks the path — no form selection required.
Predictive visibility
Each persona sees what's coming at the right horizon — deliveries this week, approvals this month, invoices next month. Every prediction shows its confidence and reasoning.
Audit-traceable compliance
12 checks run silently — sanctions, duplicates, banking, tax, grant compliance. Every agent action is timestamped and attributed, exportable for audit.
Market awareness
Commodity prices, FX moves, tariff changes, and supplier price notices automatically connect to your portfolio with quantified impact in dollars.
Pattern intelligence
The agent learns from history — benchmarking new contracts against similar past ones, detecting threshold-avoidance, forecasting close-out rushes and month-end batches.
Behavioral feedback
Where a user's action helps downstream operations, the system says so — turning invisible work into recognized contribution. Good behavior gets reinforced, not just enforced.
One agent, one composer, one audit trail — adapting its outputs to whatever the user actually needs.
Strategic category management

From buying transactions to portfolio strategy.

Procurement systems handle execution. The strategic layer — spend positioning, category plans, supplier strategy — lives in spreadsheets, disconnected from the data that should feed it. Procurello makes strategy live.

When strategy and execution live in separate tools, strategy loses. Category plans go stale the moment they're written. Managed spend targets miss because buyers never see them. The gap isn't a people problem — it's a structural one. Closing it means putting the strategic layer in the same platform as the requisition, the contract, and the invoice.

The category management module extends Procurello into that strategic layer. A fourth persona — the category manager — operates across the full portfolio: positioning spend, running sourcing events, tracking plans against KPIs, and watching managed spend move in real time as buying behaviour changes below.
