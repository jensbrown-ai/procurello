Category Brief
✨ AI Generated · 14 Mar 2025
Procurello Category Intelligence

MRO & Facilities represents Meridian Research University System's second-largest spend category at $43.8M annually, spanning 12 sites across the system. The category covers maintenance parts and components, safety equipment and PPE, janitorial and cleaning supplies, HVAC parts, tools, electrical components, and facility consumables — operationally critical inputs that have historically been purchased in a fragmented, site-by-site fashion, significantly limiting procurement's commercial leverage.

Current strategic focus is consolidation: reducing the active supplier base from 24 to a target of 15 or fewer by fiscal year-end, and driving spend under management from 61% toward the 80% annual target. The Grainger master agreement anchors approximately $14.8M of annual spend across maintenance parts and consumables, with framework contracts for safety equipment and janitorial supplies providing partial coverage in those sub-categories. However, an estimated $6.3M continues to flow through departmental P-card channels outside contracted sources — representing the single largest compliance and savings opportunity in the portfolio.

Two framework contracts require near-term renewal action: the Grainger agreement expires December 2025 and the 3M safety equipment framework expires February 2026, with no renewal sourcing events yet initiated for either. Additionally, new OSHA compliance requirements effective Q3 2025 expand PPE specifications beyond the current 3M contract scope, creating supply security obligations that must be addressed as part of the safety equipment renewal. The category's Leverage positioning on the classic matrix — combined with the Consolidate action on the Compass — reflects that the commercial advantage of aggregating $43.8M in spend across 12 sites is real, but currently underutilised.

Consolidate & Leverage — Volume Aggregation Across Sites
Reduce the active supplier base from 24 to ≤15 by end of fiscal year through competitive sourcing events and framework renegotiation. Drive compliance to contracted sources by embedding catalog buying in the Procurello requisitioning flow and implementing P-card spend controls, targeting 90% contract compliance rate. Leverage combined $43.8M spend across all 12 Meridian sites in every competitive event — volume aggregation is the primary commercial advantage in this category and it is currently being significantly underutilised due to fragmented departmental buying behaviour.

1
Increase spend under management from 61% to ≥80% by FY year-end through sourcing events covering the highest-value unmanaged sub-categories.

Deliver $380K in realized savings versus FY baseline through volume aggregation, specification standardisation, and elimination of off-contract premium pricing.

Market Intelligence
✨ AI Assisted

MRO distribution remains a highly competitive marketplace. Grainger, MSC Industrial, and Fastenal are all actively pursuing institutional accounts, with ecommerce pressure from Amazon Business suppressing list prices by 8–12% versus 2022 benchmarks. This creates a strong buyer environment for the panel renewal.

Safety equipment: 3M and Honeywell dominate the higher-education segment. Increasing compliance complexity around certification documentation is raising switching costs — a factor to address proactively in the Q3 OSHA requirement response.

Peer institution benchmarking indicates that university MRO programmes with spend above $8M that have implemented multi-site aggregated frameworks are achieving 11–16% savings versus distributed purchasing. Meridian is positioned to capture within this range on a full Grainger renewal.
